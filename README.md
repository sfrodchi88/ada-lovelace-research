## Overview

This repository gathers verified historical information on **Ada Lovelace** (1815‑1852) and her collaboration with **Charles Babbage** on the pioneering **Analytical Engine**. The material draws from authoritative sources including Wikipedia, the Computer History Museum, and the Science Museum London.

## Key Historical Figures

- Ada Lovelace – English mathematician, writer, and recognized as the first computer programmer.
- Charles Babbage – Inventor of the Difference Engine and the Analytical Engine, often called the "father of the computer".
- Luigi Menabrea – Italian engineer whose 1842 paper described the Analytical Engine and was later translated by Lovelace.
- Mary Somerville – Scientist and tutor who introduced Lovelace to Babbage.

## Museum Resources Found

- **Computer History Museum (Mountain View, CA)** – Exhibits on Babbage’s Difference and Analytical Engines; collections include a partial mill model.
- **Science Museum London** – Displays parts of Babbage’s engines; artifacts and detailed documentation are available through their online catalogue.
